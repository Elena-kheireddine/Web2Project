Learn more about yoga

Yoga focuses on your breathing, strength, flexibility, & balance.

  • In yoga, there is no 'good' or 'bad', 'easy' or 'hard, 'beginner' or 'advanced'. The beauty of yoga lies in its diversity and adaptability. We can modify the practice's length, use props, and choose different styles, on or off the mat.
  • Yoga is about how we feel in the moment, and it's not about comparing ourselves to others. One's yoga journey is unique and personal. The hardest part for many is finding stillness and peace in Savasana, a true challenge and triumph.
  • Let's move beyond the labels of beginner and advanced and embrace the individuality of each practice. Whatever your yoga looks like today, it is enough because it is yours - a personal and fulfilling journey. Embrace it wholeheartedly.

How yoga works for yoga practitioners?

Yoga poses: work your muscles, joints, and connective tissues to increase strength, mobility and balance. It's is a low impact, full body movement practice at its most basic level.

Breath control: helps you become aware of your breath & how to regulate it. This can increase your lung capacity, oxygen intake, mental clarity, and relaxation response.

Mindfulness: Practicing yoga increases your mind-body connection by helping you become more aware of your sensations, thoughts, and emotions. It can lead to improve self-awareness, emotional regulation, and overall well-being

Resilience: Practicing yoga can improve pain tolerance and resilience. Regular yoga practice was associated with increased gray matter volume in brain regions involved in pain regulation, emotional processing, and self-awareness.

Mental Health: Yoga is a powerful tool for reducing stress and promoting relaxation. Studies have shown that practicing yoga can lower cortisol levels (a hormone associated with stress), reduce anxiety and depression symptoms, and improve overall well-being.

Letting Go: Yoga can help you become mindful of your body's sensations and feelings, allowing you to identify and release tension and emotions that may be held in the body. That may have happened to you if you felt emotional release happens in poses like pigeon or backbends.

OM

Our classes end with a final relaxation followed by the chant OM. It is a symbol of peace, tranquility, and unity. It reminds us to slow down and breathe. It is an invitation to listen to our own voice and connect deeply to the vibrations coming from within ourselves. These vibrations are calming to the mind and the central nervous system.


Benefits of OM:

  • Reducing stress levels
  • Improving concentration
  • Regulating sleep cycles
  • letting go of negativity
  • feeling energized and invigorated
