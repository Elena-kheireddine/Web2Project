Yin Yoga

In Yin yoga, the poses are held for a long period of time (typically three to five minutes or longer) to target the connective tissues- ligaments, joints, bones, the deep fascia networks of the body and the meridians rather than focusing on the muscles.


The major benefits of Yin Yoga:

  • Sretches and restores flexibility to the connective tissues and muscles of the body
  • Improves base-line flexibility
  • Improves range of motion
  • Reduces adhesions
  • Creates therapeutic stress on the joints
  • Improves organ health
  • Trains the mind to stay calm and quiet in the face of internal and external stresses
  • Reverses the aging process on the spine
  • Enhances and deepens meditation practice
  • Brings emotional balance to body, mind, and spirit
  • Helps to prevent fusion of the joints
  • Increases mobility and lubricates the joints

  • Calms the nervous system
  • Slows the mind
  • Decrease stress
  • Balances life force energy
  • Lengthens muscles
  • Hydrates tissues and muscles
  • Thickens and strengthens ligaments
  • Lubricates joints
  • Hydrates skin
  • Detoxifies the body
  • Reduces degeneration of tissues
  • Normalizes the curvature of the spine
